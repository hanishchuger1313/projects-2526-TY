After winning the toss and opting to bowl first, Zimbabwe’s bowlers Blessing Muzarabani and Richard Ngarava kept things tight from the beginning and never allowed Oman to settle.

The pressure resulted in regular wickets, with Muzarabani, Ngarava, and Brad Evans picking up three wickets each to dismantle Oman’s batting. Vinayak Shukla top-scored with 28 runs, while Sufyan Mehmood made 25 runs and Nadeem Khan added 20 runs, the only batters to reach double figures as Oman were bowled out for 103 runs in 19.5 overs.


In reply, Zimbabwe made the chase look comfortable from the start. Tadiwanashe Marumani provided a positive opening, while Brian Bennett anchored the innings with an unbeaten 48 runs off 36 balls.

Brendan Taylor contributed a steady 31 runs before retiring hurt with a hamstring injury, briefly slowing the momentum. For Oman, Sufyan Mehmood was the standout bowler, finishing with an economical spell of 2 for 12.

Zimbabwe reached the target in 13.3 overs, finishing at 106 for 2 to seal a convincing win with 39 balls to spare. The victory earned them two points and a strong net run rate of +1.450, which could prove important later in the tournament.
